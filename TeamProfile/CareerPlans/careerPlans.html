Career plans changes: Data Analytics (Nathan)

One area I am interested in is Augmented Analytics, although when researching it it seems to be often described as simply Data Science. One thing that is certain with the vast internet of things is that the amount of data is constantly expanding. I am particularly interested in the stock market and how algorithms trade with other algorithms to make money. One course I was also looking at before choosing IIT was financial planning. I like to dabble a little in the stock market but trade for the long term. I would like to learn how to apply algorithms and try automated investing and day trading.

I like how it can also involve a bit of programming and see the language R is mentioned often. There are a multitude of Introductory lessons online to learn R which is a popular language designed for data science. The next course after this will be the Introduction to Programming which will help me in choosing how to pursue this.

Team Leader, Data and Analytics Job in Sydney - SEEK

Here is an absolutely awesome job working for the government at the top of the financial chain - The Reserve Bank. Being that I am already employed for the local government I understand that it will be a tough job, working hard, non-stop, short lunches and long days with management looking over your shoulder. Stop laughing, it's true.

There is no better place to earn money than at the place that prints it.

The Reserve Bank analyses swathes of data from the economy (particularly the financial sector) in order to figure out where to put (or restrict) the federal governments influence. The Reserve Banks influence on the economy has been likened to steering an oil tanker. It can take a long time for the effects to be noticed.

The Reserve Bank is responsible for keeping the financial system honest and looking out for problems before they happen. This looks like it is a new department also and would be fantastic to start on the ground floor setting up systems to automatically interpret the data looking for anomalies.
